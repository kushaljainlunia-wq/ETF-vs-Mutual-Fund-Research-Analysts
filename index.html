About This Project

Welcome to my ETF vs Mutual Fund Research terminal, a project developed to perform structured and comprehensive analysis of individual ETFs and Mutual Fund schemes through live web data verification.


The research report integrates scheme-wise performance analysis (XIRR, returns, benchmark/category comparison), ETF vs MF comparison (13 features), ETF vs Index Fund clarifier (India-specific), hidden costs table (brokerage, demat, STT, slippage), SIP compatibility check (ETF ❌ vs MF ✅), tracking error trend chart (1Y/3Y), liquidity heatmap for ETFs (volume, bid-ask, AUM), tax bracket simulator (equity + debt by income), cost comparison (TER vs peers), better alternatives in the same category, action recommendations (Buy/Don't Buy/Wait/Swap), tax optimization, manager/issuer track record, AMC/issuer strength, and a 3-tier scoring framework into an interactive analytical framework.


The objective of this project is to transform ETF/MF data into meaningful investment insights and establish a standardized approach for evaluating individual ETFs and Mutual Fund schemes before investing or swapping.


Through this initiative, I aim to demonstrate the application of financial analysis, ETF/MF expertise, data visualization, and analytical problem-solving in the field of ETF and Mutual Fund research and investment decision-making.

🔍 Recommended Workflow

The most effective approach is to use this research terminal alongside mutual fund platforms, ETF screeners, and stock platforms (like Screener.in).

  1. Use mutual fund/ETF platforms or Screener.in to discover and shortlist potential scheme opportunities.
  2. Provide the ETF symbol or MF name to the research terminal (e.g., "Nifty 50 ETF" or "Parag Parikh Flexi Cap - Direct Growth").
  3. Understand fund/issuer quality, cost efficiency (including hidden costs), manager/issuer execution, peer comparison, SIP compatibility, tracking error, liquidity, and tax impact.
  4. Review ETF vs MF comparison and ETF vs Index Fund clarifier (if considering passive investing).
  5. Review top 3 better alternatives in the same category/index (if available).
  6. Review action recommendations (Buy/Don't Buy/Wait/Swap) and tax considerations.
  7. Use the consolidated insights and scheme score to support investment decisions.
